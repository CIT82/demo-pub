To enhance my indoor cycling experience, I successfully "hacked" my Peloton bike to bypass its proprietary ecosystem and explore the expansive virtual landscapes of Rouvy. This project involved utilizing a third-party power meter or a "bridge" device to broadcast my cadence and power data via Bluetooth/ANT+ to an external tablet, effectively turning the Peloton into a high-end stationary trainer for open-world simulations. By sideloading or utilizing an external screen, I shifted from instructor-led studio classes to real-world augmented reality routes, allowing for a more personalized and data-driven training regimen. This transition highlights the flexibility of modern fitness hardware when integrated with cross-platform software solutions.
